# Brutal Zombie Horde Survival — Crafting Recipe Guide

A complete guide to every crafting recipe in the mod, organized by category. All recipes use **vanilla crafting stations** — there are no custom workstations. Craft at a Crafting Table, smelt in a Blast Furnace, cook on a Campfire, or smelt in a Furnace.

---

## How Crafting Works

After the workstation rationalization, all mod recipes use vanilla Minecraft crafting mechanics:

| Station | Type | What It Does |
|---------|------|--------------|
| **Crafting Table** | Shapeless / Shaped | Most mod recipes — weapons, medicine, food, materials, building, electricity |
| **Blast Furnace** | Blasting | Smelting metals — Iron Scrap → Iron Ingot, Iron Ingot → Forged Iron, Lead → Forged Lead |
| **Campfire** | Campfire Cooking | Purifying Murky Water → Boiled Water, roasting Corn on the Cob |
| **Furnace** | Smelting | Purifying Murky Water → Boiled Water (alternative to campfire) |

All mod recipes appear in the **vanilla Recipe Book** and can be unlocked through normal gameplay advancements.

---

## Vanilla Recipe Overrides

The mod **disables** four vanilla smelting recipes to prevent easy nugget farming from gear:

| Disabled Recipe | Reason |
|-----------------|--------|
| Iron Nugget from Smelting (armor/tools) | Prevents trivial iron recovery |
| Iron Nugget from Blasting (armor/tools) | Prevents trivial iron recovery |
| Gold Nugget from Smelting (armor/tools) | Prevents trivial gold recovery |
| Gold Nugget from Blasting (armor/tools) | Prevents trivial gold recovery |


```
Collect water with Glass Bottle → Auto-converts to Murky Water
    ↓
Cook Murky Water at Campfire or Furnace → Boiled Water
    ↓

```

## 🗺️ Early-Game Crafting Roadmap

Here's a recommended crafting priority for your first few in-game days:

### Day 1 — Survival Basics
1. **Stone Club** (Cobblestone ×3, Stick ×2) — your first weapon
2. **Campfire** (vanilla recipe) — cook food and purify water
3. **Boiled Water** — purify Murky Water immediately to avoid Dysentery
4. **Bandage** (Paper ×2, String) — stop Bleeding from zombie hits

### Day 2–3 — Establish Resources
5. **Splint** (Stick ×2, String) — cure Sprains and Fractures
6. **Upgradeable Frame** (Oak Planks ×4) — start building your base
7. **Concrete Mix** (Sand ×2, Cobblestone ×2) — strengthen your base walls
8. Build or find a **Blast Furnace** — start smelting iron

### Day 4–7 — Mid-Game Progression
9. **Forged Iron** (blast Iron Ingots) — unlocks most advanced recipes
10. **Nails** (Forged Iron ×2 → ×4) — needed for Baseball Bat
11. **Baseball Bat** (Oak Planks ×3, Nail ×2, Duct Tape) — solid melee upgrade
12. **Gunpowder** (Nitrate ×2, Coal) — prepare for firearms
13. **9mm Pistol** (Forged Iron ×3, Mechanical Parts ×2, Spring) — first ranged weapon
14. **9mm Ammo** (Gunpowder, Forged Lead) — keep your Pistol fed

### Late Game — Advanced Crafting
15. **Forged Steel** (Forged Iron ×5, Mechanical Parts ×3) — top-tier material
16. **AK-47** (Forged Steel ×4, Mechanical Parts ×3, Spring ×2) — best weapon
17. **Generator/Solar/Battery** — power your base defenses
18. **Antibiotics → First Aid Kit** — endgame healing

## 💡 Tips

- **All recipes are shapeless** except Upgradeable Frame (2×2 shaped) — just throw ingredients in any slot.
- **Water purification** works at both Campfire and Furnace — keep one running at all times.
- **Forged Iron is the keystone material** — once you start blasting Iron Ingots, most of the crafting tree opens up.
- **Mechanical Parts are the bottleneck** — they're used in Forged Steel, all firearms, and all power blocks. Scrap tools and loot carefully.
- **Oil Shale is desert-exclusive** — you need it for Polymer (medicine, ammo), Gas Can (generators), and Forged Lead (bullets). Plan desert expeditions.
- **Nitrate is everywhere** — it's used in Gunpowder, Acid, Fertilizer, AND Antibiotics. Stock up.
- **Cook stews, not individual ingredients** — stews give both fullness AND hydration, making them the most efficient food recipes.
- **The Recipe Book works** — all mod recipes unlock through normal advancements and are searchable in the vanilla recipe book UI.
